Workflow

Design Process

From raw geometry to cinematic final render — a step-by-step look at how I build photorealistic scenes. Project: Gondor — The White City.

01

Clay Model

Base geometry and massing. Pure 3D modeling without materials — focusing on proportions, scale, and architectural detail.

02

Vegetation

Adding organic elements — trees, grass, ground cover. Integrating nature with the built environment to create realism.

03

Environment

Full scene assembly — sky, water, terrain, and atmospheric effects. Building the complete world around the architecture.

04

Composition

Camera placement, lighting design, and material refinement. Crafting the cinematic framing that tells the story.

05

Final Render

High-resolution render with Corona Renderer, followed by color grading and post-production. The complete vision realized.

About

The Artist Behind the Vision

I'm Andrew Montejo, an architect and 3D visualization specialist based in Lima, Peru, with over 8 years of experience transforming architectural blueprints into compelling photorealistic imagery.

My process is meticulous: every project goes through careful 3D modeling, scene composition, lighting design, material crafting, high-resolution rendering, and professional post-production — crafted to communicate architectural intent with cinematic clarity.

Current and recent client work covers luxury brands across watchmaking, retail, and beauty (under NDA), alongside residential, hospitality and commercial projects for architecture studios and developers worldwide.
